Causes

Learn about Cyber Stalking
  • Cyberstalking is more common than you might think. About 26% of women and 14% of men have been cyberstalked at some point in their lives.
  • Cyberstalking can happen to anyone. There is no one type of person who is more likely to be cyberstalked.
  • Cyberstalkers can be anyone. They may be someone you know, such as an ex-partner or a classmate, or they may be someone you have never met.
  • Cyberstalking can have a serious impact on the victim's mental and emotional health. Victims of cyberstalking are more likely to experience anxiety, depression, and PTSD.
Learn about Doxing

Outing/doxxing is when someone shares your private information online without your permission. This can include things like your real name, address, phone number, or social media accounts.

Outing/doxxing can be used to harass, intimidate, or threaten someone. It can also be used to ruin someone's reputation or make it difficult for them to live their life.

Here are some examples of outing/doxxing:
  • Sharing someone's home address online without their permission.
  • Posting someone's phone number online and encouraging others to call or text them.
  • Sharing someone's social media accounts online and encouraging others to harass them.
  • Posting someone's personal information on a website or forum.
Learn about Online Harassment

Online harassment is when someone uses the internet to bully, threaten, or embarrass you. It can happen on social media, in chat rooms, or even in online games.

Online harassment can be just as hurtful as face-to-face bullying, and it can have a serious impact on your mental and emotional health.

Here are some examples of online harassment:
  • Sending you mean or threatening messages.
  • Posting embarrassing photos or videos of you online without your permission.
  • Spreading rumors about you online.
  • Hacking into your social media accounts or email.
  • Making fun of you or calling you names online.
